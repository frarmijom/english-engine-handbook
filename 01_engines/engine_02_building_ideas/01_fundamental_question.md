# How does English build ideas?

Learning individual words is not enough to communicate.

Imagine that you know these words:

```
engineer

server

restart
```

You understand every word.

However, no complete idea exists yet.

Now look at this:

```
The engineer restarted the server.
```

The words did not change.

Only their organization changed.

Meaning appeared.

This is one of the most important characteristics of English.

English is not simply a collection of words.

English is a system that organizes ideas.

This Engine explains how those ideas are built.

You will not begin by studying grammar.

You will begin by understanding the natural architecture behind every English sentence.

By the end of this Engine, you should no longer ask:

> "What grammar rule should I use?"

Instead, you should naturally ask:

> "What idea do I want to build?"

That small change is the beginning of thinking in English.
